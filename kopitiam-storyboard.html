Art Direction

🏪 The Stall

Classic kopitiam drinks stall: marble counter, cup rack with porcelain cups upside-down, sock filters (kopi sock) hanging from hooks, rows of condensed milk cans with two punched holes, hot water urn with steam, tall glasses for iced drinks. Everything has the patina of decades of use.

🪑 The Setting

Overhead: slow ceiling fan (always). Fluorescent tube lighting mixed with warm incandescent bulbs. Green-and-white formica tables visible in background. Red plastic stools. Tissue paper packets "choping" seats. Morning newspaper on a table. Old TV on a shelf playing the news.

🎨 Style: Warm Cartoon

Colorful but grounded 2D illustration. Not pixel art — smooth lines with warm shading. Think "Overcooked" meets local flavor. Characters are slightly exaggerated Singaporean archetypes drawn with affection. Food and drinks should look appetizing — the condensed milk pour must make people crave kopi.

🧦 The Sock Filter

The iconic kopi sock is the visual centerpiece. Long cloth filter on a wire frame, stained dark brown from years of use. When brewing, coffee pours through in a thick dark stream. The sock should feel like a character itself — the tool that makes kopitiam coffee different from everything else.

☕ Liquid Physics

Drinks should look satisfying. Coffee is dark and opaque. Condensed milk is thick and golden — it sinks and swirls. Evaporated milk is lighter, creamier. Tea is amber-brown. Ice cubes clink and bob. Teh Tarik streams in a frothy arc between cups. The Milo Dinosaur has visible powder sprinkled on top.

🗣️ Singlish Flavor

All text uses natural Singlish: "Can!", "Shiok!", "Wah, power lah", "Aiyah, wrong one". Customer speech bubbles feel like actual kopitiam conversation. Menu items in English + Chinese characters. Order tickets look like real kopitiam chits — cream paper, handwritten-style font.

Audio Atmosphere

🎵
Ambient Layer
Ceiling fan hum, distant conversation murmur, occasional bird outside, clinking porcelain, the muted thud of cups on marble, newspaper rustling
🎶
Gameplay Music
Lo-fi chill with Singaporean flavor — light xylophone, gentle bass, maybe a bit of guzheng or erhu melody woven in. Tempo rises during rush hour. Drops to calm between shifts.
☕
Drink-Making SFX
Coffee sock squeeze (cloth wringing), thick pour of condensed milk (glug), sugar spoon tink, ice cubes dropping into glass (clink clink), teh tarik arc (swoosh), hot water pour (ssssh), Milo powder sprinkle (soft rain)
😊
Customer Reactions
Happy: "Wah, shiok!" / "Can, uncle!" / "Steady lah!" — Impatient: "Eh faster can?" / *tsk* / tapping on counter — Wrong drink: "Aiyoh, this one wrong leh" / "Uncle, I order teh, not kopi!"
🎯
Reward Sounds
Perfect drink: satisfying cash register cha-ching + crowd murmur. Streak milestone: rising xylophone scale. Shift complete: closing shutter rattle + evening crickets. Star earned: bright chime.

Kopitiam Details

The magic is in the details that Singaporeans will instantly recognize:

  • Condensed milk cans with two triangular holes punched in the top — always two
  • The sock filter (kopi kao) stained permanently brown, cloth stretched over a wire frame with a long handle
  • Porcelain cups — the thick white ones, slightly chipped, stacked upside-down
  • Tall glasses for iced drinks with red or green striped straws
  • Metal spoons in a cup on the counter
  • The pour technique — raising the sock high to aerate the coffee as it streams down
  • Order chits — small pieces of cream paper with drink codes written in pen
  • Marble counter with grey-green veins, worn smooth from decades
  • Pricing board — hand-painted white text on red or green background
  • Plastic bags for takeaway drinks — kopi in a bag with straw poking out
  • Tissue paper packets on tables — the universal seat reservation system
