Board 5A

Consequence — Red Flag Warning

When the player makes a risky choice, the game doesn't punish — it educates. A narrative consequence plays out, followed by a "Red Flag" card explaining the real-world danger signal the player missed.

⚠️
Red Flag Detected

Mali packs quickly and leaves with Khun Prasert the next morning. On the bus, he takes her ID card "for safekeeping."

When they arrive, it's not a restaurant — it's a karaoke bar. There are other young women here. The doors have locks on the outside.

Mali realizes she doesn't know anyone in this city. She has no phone, no ID, and no money.

🚩 Real-World Red Flags
Urgency pressure — "Leave tomorrow or lose the chance" prevents you from verifying.

Taking documents — A legitimate employer never needs to hold your ID.

No written contract — Real jobs have written terms you can review first.

Player can rewind and try a safer choice — learning through consequence, not failure

Art Direction

🎨 Style: Flat Illustration

Warm, approachable flat illustration with limited details. Similar to "Florence" or "Alto's Adventure." Avoids photorealism to prevent triggering trauma. Silhouettes and color convey mood.

🌏 Setting: Pan-SE Asian

Architecture and clothing reference real SE Asian cultures but avoid stereotyping any one country. Temple silhouettes, tropical vegetation, market stalls, and monsoon skies establish location.

💡 Light as Metaphor

Warm golden light = safety, home, truth. Cool blue/purple = uncertainty, danger, deception. Red accents = immediate threat, urgency. Green = safe choices, positive outcomes.

📱 Mobile-First Layout

Top 55% = scene art (visual storytelling). Bottom 45% = dialogue/choices (interaction). One-thumb navigation. Large tap targets (48px min). Text scales for accessibility. RTL-ready layout.

Design Principles

01
Educate, don't traumatize
Show consequences without graphic content. Use implication and emotion, not shock.
02
Agency over helplessness
Players always have a path to safety. Rewind mechanics mean no permanent failure states.
03
Respect, not rescue narratives
Characters are smart people in difficult situations — not victims to be saved. Choices reflect real agency.
04
Culturally specific, universally accessible
Stories reflect real patterns in each country. UI works across literacy levels with visual cues.
05
Offline-first, share-native
Full gameplay without internet. Share button is a core feature, not an afterthought.
